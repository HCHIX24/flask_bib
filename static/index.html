NUESTRA COLECION

The Body Keeps the Score

Bessel van der Kolk explores how trauma affects the brain and body, shaping one's emotional and physical well-being. It offers insights into innovative healing approaches, such as mindfulness, yoga, and neurofeedback, to help individuals recover from trauma.

Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents

Isabel Wilkerson examines the deeply entrenched system of caste in America, drawing parallels between the racial hierarchy in the U.S., the caste systems of India, and Nazi Germany. Wilkerson explores how caste shapes individuals' lives and societal structures, highlighting its impact on inequality, discrimination, and systemic injustice.

Think Again: The Power of Knowing What You Don't Know

Adam Grant encourages readers to embrace intellectual humility and rethink their assumptions. It highlights the importance of questioning one's beliefs, staying open to new ideas, and learning from others in order to make better decisions and foster personal and professional growth.

Becoming Supernatural

Dr. Joe Dispenza explores the intersection of science and spirituality, revealing how individuals can transcend their physical limitations and create extraordinary changes in their lives. Through practical techniques, including meditation and mindfulness, Dispenza shows how to rewire the brain and body to access higher levels of consciousness, healing, and transformation.

Can't Hurt Me: Master Your Mind and Defy the Odds

David Goggins is a memoir and self-help guide that chronicles the author's journey from a challenging, abusive childhood to becoming a Navy SEAL and ultramarathon runner. Through his story, Goggins shares powerful lessons on mental toughness, resilience, and overcoming self-imposed limits to achieve extraordinary success.

The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck

Mark Manson challenges conventional self-help advice by emphasizing the importance of embracing life's difficulties and focusing on what truly matters. Through blunt and humorous language, Manson encourages readers to let go of societal expectations and prioritize their values for a more meaningful, fulfilling life.

12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos

Jordan Peterson offers practical advice grounded in psychology, philosophy, and personal anecdotes to help individuals navigate the complexities of life. Each of the 12 rules addresses a fundamental aspect of human existence, emphasizing responsibility, meaning, and order in the face of chaos and suffering.

Greenlights

McConaughey is a memoir that combines personal anecdotes, life lessons, and unconventional wisdom drawn from the actor's experiences. Through a mix of humor and insight, McConaughey reflects on his journey, offering readers a guide to navigating life's challenges with authenticity and resilience.

The Five Dysfunctions of a Team

Patrick Lencioni outlines the common challenges that prevent teams from working effectively together. It identifies five key dysfunctions—absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, and inattention to results—and offers actionable strategies for overcoming these obstacles to build cohesive and high-performing teams.

Dare to Lead

Brené Brown focuses on the power of vulnerability and courage in leadership. Brown emphasizes the importance of building trust, embracing vulnerability, and creating a culture of empathy to foster strong, effective teams.
